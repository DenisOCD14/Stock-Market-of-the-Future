Retirement

Planning for a 100-year life.

As lifespans stretch and the "retire at 65" model fades, retirement is becoming less a finish line and more a long, flexible chapter. Here's how to fund it.

The new reality

Why the old playbook needs an update

Three shifts mean today's savers can't simply copy their grandparents' plan.

Longer lives

A pension may need to last 30–40 years, not 15. Your money has to keep growing well into retirement.

Fluid careers

Phased retirement, encore careers, and gig income blur the line between working and retired.

You bear the risk

As guaranteed pensions fade, the responsibility — and the upside — sits with the individual saver.

Your roadmap

A plan you can actually follow.

You don't need to predict the future — you need a plan robust enough to survive it. Start with these moves.

  • Start early — decades of compounding do the heavy lifting
  • Capture every employer match; it's an instant, guaranteed return
  • Use tax-advantaged accounts before taxable ones
  • Glide from growth toward stability as you approach the date
  • Plan a withdrawal strategy so savings outlast you, not the other way round
The power of starting early
Illustrative growth of steady contributions

Compounding starts slow and accelerates. The curve rewards the years you can't get back — which is why starting today beats starting bigger later.
